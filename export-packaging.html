Export Packaging Requirements: What You Need to Know

Any wood packaging material — pallets, crates, dunnage, or blocking — used in international shipments must comply with ISPM-15 phytosanitary standards. This applies to virtually every country you're shipping to: the European Union, China, Japan, Australia, Brazil, and more.

Non-compliant wood packaging is one of the most common reasons for cargo delays at international ports. Inspectors check for the IPPC mark on every piece of wood packaging. If it's missing — or if the wood appears untreated — your container can be held, re-packaged at your expense, or returned.

The simplest way to stay compliant is to source all of your export packaging from a certified manufacturer. When you order pallets or crates from Victor Wood Products for an international shipment, you receive ISPM-15 compliant products stamped with our IPPC registration mark — ready for customs inspection at any port in the world.

Quick compliance checklist for exporters:

  • All pallets are ISPM-15 heat treated and stamped with IPPC mark
  • All crates are built from certified heat treated lumber
  • Dunnage and blocking material is also ISPM-15 treated (not just the pallet)
  • Documentation is available from your manufacturer if requested by customs
