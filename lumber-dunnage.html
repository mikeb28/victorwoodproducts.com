What Is Dunnage?

Dunnage is any loose material used to protect, stabilize, or separate cargo during shipping. In the context of ocean container and flatbed truck shipping, wood dunnage refers to lumber pieces placed under, around, or between cargo to prevent movement, damage, and contact with the container floor or walls.

Dunnage serves a few key functions: it elevates cargo off the container floor to allow forklift access, it fills voids between cargo units to prevent shifting during transit, and it absorbs vibration and impact that would otherwise be transmitted directly to your goods.

Softwood vs. Hardwood: Which Do You Need?

The right material depends on the load you're securing and the application:

Softwood (Southern Yellow Pine, SPF)

Best for most dunnage applications. Lighter, easier to cut, and cost-effective. Adequate strength for standard cargo separation and blocking in containers and trailers. Most common choice for freight forwarder and port use.

Hardwood (Oak, Maple, Mixed)

Higher density and load-bearing capacity. Use hardwood blocking when you're securing very heavy cargo — steel coils, machinery, large fabrications — where softwood may compress or split under load. Also preferred for repeated-use blocking applications.

Not sure which to use? Call us — we'll ask a few questions about your cargo and recommend the right material.
